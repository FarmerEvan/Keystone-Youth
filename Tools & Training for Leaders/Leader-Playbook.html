🚧 Expectations & Boundaries

Why Youth Events?

Serving within the local church is not merely a volunteer activity; it is the natural expression of the metaphor of the Body found in 1 Corinthians 12. When we serve, we move from being consumers of the Gospel to participants in the Great Commission. For the youth, this shifts their identity from being "the future of the church" to being active, vital members of the present church. By engaging in student service, they "stir up one another to love and good works" (Hebrews 10:24), anchoring their faith in action.

⏰ Sunday Logistics

Please make it a priority to be at the huddle before we begin at 5:00pm. During fellowship times, let’s be present and keep an eye out for anyone who may feel left out. Help make the atmosphere warm, welcoming, and intentional.

🛡️ Safety & Relational Boundaries

We encourage you to connect with our youth during the week (lunch at school, sporting events, plays). However, we caution you to avoid being alone with the youth at any point and time.

To maintain healthy boundaries, we ask that females connect with females and males with males. In-person conversations with mixed genders are encouraged, but please be mindful when discussing sensitive topics. One-on-one conversations with the opposite sex about sensitive matters should be avoided. Please include another person.

🚨 Reporting Concerns

Keep staff informed about any concerns so we can determine appropriate next steps. This includes: relationship issues, self-harm, unhealthy boundaries, questions around gender identity, and similar matters.

🤝 Biblical Conflict Approach

Disagreements happen, but how we handle them matters. We follow a biblical model for resolving conflict:

  • Step 1: Private ConfrontationMatthew 18:15-17 outlines the process: talk to the person alone first, then with witnesses if needed, and finally with church leadership to bring resolution.
  • Step 2: Prompt ReconciliationMatthew 5:23-24 advises settling issues immediately, even pausing worship to reconcile.
  • Step 3: Peace & Unity is the GoalRomans 12:18 advises, "If it is possible, as far as it depends on you, live at peace with everyone."

Principles of Peace

Controlling Anger & Speech: Ephesians 4:26-27 warns against letting the sun go down on anger. Proverbs 17:14 suggests dropping a matter before it blows up.
Gentle Correction: 2 Timothy 2:24-25 emphasizes that leaders should not quarrel but be gentle and patient with opponents.
Handling Accusations: 1 Timothy 5:19-20 instructs that accusations against leaders require two or three witnesses to ensure fairness.
