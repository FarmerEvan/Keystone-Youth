🌍 Why Youth Events?

The Biblical Mandate for Service and Youth

Serving within the local church is not merely a volunteer activity; it is the natural expression of the metaphor of the Body found in 1 Corinthians 12. Paul argues that "the eye cannot say to the hand, ‘I have no need of you’" (1 Corinthians 12:21), implying that every member, regardless of age, possesses a spiritual gift intended for the "common good" (12:7).

When we serve, we move from being consumers of the Gospel to participants in the Great Commission (Matthew 28:19-20). For the youth, this is profoundly beneficial because it shifts their identity from being "the future of the church" to being active, vital members of the present church.

By engaging in student service, they "stir up one another to love and good works" (Hebrews 10:24), which anchors their faith in action and protects them from the "drifting away" that often occurs when faith remains purely academic. Whether serving is leading a song, helping a younger peer, or participating in a challenge that illustrates a spiritual truth, it is all vital to their spiritual formation.
