The Grand Story:

Out of Egypt

Discovering the Great "I AM"

Youth Group Weekly Take-Home
Covering: Exodus 1-3

Where Are We?

After a 400-year gap between Genesis and Exodus, the Israelites went from being honored guests in Egypt to suffering slaves. They cried out, and God heard them!

The Burning Bush

God called a runaway shepherd named Moses from a bush that was on fire but wouldn't burn up. When Moses asked God for His name, God said: "I AM WHO I AM."

  • He is eternal.
  • He needs nothing from anyone else.
  • He is the Promise-Keeper.

The Jesus Connection

In John 8:58, Jesus shocked the leaders by saying, "Before Abraham was born, I AM!" Jesus was claiming the name of God from the burning bush. Moses rescued people from physical slavery; Jesus rescues us from spiritual slavery!

Mid-Week Dive Deep

Grab a parent, a sibling, or just your Bible and a notebook, and take 10 minutes to work through this!

1. READ IT: Read Exodus 3:13-15 together.

For Younger Kids:

Imagine walking in the desert and seeing a talking, burning bush. What is the first thing you would do? Why did God use something so wild to get Moses' attention?

For Older Youth:

Moses was terrified and full of excuses. Have you ever felt like God was asking you to do something too hard or scary? How does knowing God is the great "I AM" help you overcome that fear?

Weekly Action Steps

Choose your challenge for the week:

The "Silent Gap" Challenge

The Israelites waited 400 years for rescue. Is there something you've been praying for that feels ignored? Write it on a sticky note for your mirror. Every time you see it, say: "God has not forgotten me."

The "I AM" Walk

Go for a 10-minute walk. As you walk, thank God for who He is. (Example: "God, thank you that You are strong. Thank you that You are always with me.")

The Jesus Connection

Read John 8:12. Jesus says, "I am the light of the world." Pick one specific way to show God's love and be a light to someone else at school this week.
