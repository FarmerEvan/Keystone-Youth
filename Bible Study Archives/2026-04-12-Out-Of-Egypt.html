Out of Egypt

The Groan, The Rescue, and The Great "I AM" | Exodus 1-3

Message Notes & Key Points

Hey Keystone Youth! In this study, we are looking at the roughly 400-year gap between Genesis and Exodus, the birth of Moses, and his encounter with God at the burning bush.

The Ultimate Promise: When Moses made excuses and said "I can't," God didn't list off Moses' good qualities. God simply said, "I will be with you" (Exodus 3:12).

Things to Pay Attention To:

  • God Hears the Groans: The Israelites waited in slavery for 400 years. It felt like God was silent, but He saw their oppression, remembered His promises, and acted in His perfect timing. God has not forgotten you!
  • Our Way vs. God's Way: Moses tried to be a rescuer in his own strength by murdering the Egyptian taskmaster. When we try to fix our problems in our own flesh, it always backfires. True deliverance requires God's power.
  • The Jesus Connection: God revealed His name to Moses as "I AM." Centuries later, Jesus shocked the religious leaders by looking at them and declaring, "Before Abraham was born, I AM!" (John 8:58). Moses was sent to rescue people from physical slavery, but Jesus came to rescue us from spiritual slavery!
