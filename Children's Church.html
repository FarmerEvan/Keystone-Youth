Joseph the Dream Helper

April 12, 2026  •  Leader: Jeff Westerhold

Now that we have celebrated the joy of Easter, we will return to our story of Joseph. This lesson shows us that even in a difficult place, God was still with Joseph and was using him to help others. We will keep our Easter theme for the month to continue celebrating that Jesus is alive!

🎯 Lesson Overview

This Month's Big Picture: Jesus is alive! ✝️

Memory Verse: "He has risen!" - Matthew 28:6
(Motions: "He has risen!" - start crouched down low, then jump up with hands in the air.)


Today's Main Idea: God helped Joseph help others.

Today's Big Question: Have you ever had a dream?

Today's Scripture: Genesis 40

📖 Welcome & Bible Story

Welcome, Playtime & Worship

Arrival: As children arrive, have simple puzzles or shape-sorting toys available for them to play with.

Worship Songs: Gather the children for a joyful time of worship. Let's sing "This Little Light of Mine"!

Story Time Script

Tell this story with a focus on Joseph's special ability from God.

Leader: "Do you remember our friend Joseph? His brothers had sent him far away to Egypt. It was a sad time, but was Joseph all alone? No! God was with him."
"Joseph was in a place called prison. While he was there, he made two new friends: a cupbearer, who gave the king his drinks, and a baker, who made the king's bread."
"One night, both friends had very strange dreams, and it made them sad and confused. They didn't know what their dreams meant."
"But Joseph knew who could help! God could! God helped Joseph understand the dreams. Joseph told the cupbearer that his dream meant something happy would happen for him soon. He told the baker that his dream meant something sad."
"Even in prison, God was with Joseph, giving him a special gift to understand dreams and help the people around him."

🎨 Activities & Games

Craft: Helping Hands

Materials: Construction paper, crayons, and stickers.

Instructions: Trace each child's hand on a piece of construction paper and help them cut it out (or have them pre-cut). Let them decorate their "helping hand" with crayons and stickers.

Teaching Point: As they decorate, say, "God gave Joseph special gifts to be a helper. God gives us hands to be helpers, too! We can help our friends and family."

Game: Dream Charades

Instructions: Act out a simple "dream" for the children (like pretending to fly like a bird or eat a giant cookie). Have them guess what you are dreaming about. Let brave children have a turn acting out a simple dream, too.

Teaching Point: This is a fun, imaginative game that connects to the story. "Joseph's friends had confusing dreams, but God helped Joseph understand them!"

🥨 Snack Time & Wrap-up

Snack Idea: The "Cupbearer and Baker" snack: a small cup of juice and a cracker or piece of bread. (Note: There is grape juice in the fridge and pretzels available in the room).

Review: While they eat, ask simple questions:
• "What did Joseph help his friends with?" (Their dreams!)
• "Who was helping Joseph?" (God!)

Closing Prayer:
"Dear God, thank you for always being with us, just like you were with Joseph. Thank you for giving us gifts so we can help others. Amen."
