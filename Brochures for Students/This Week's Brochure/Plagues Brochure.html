The Grand Story:

The Plagues

Power, Pride, & The Finger of God

Youth Group Weekly Take-Home
Covering: Exodus 4-11

Where Are We?

Moses returned to Egypt to demand Israel's freedom. But Pharaoh arrogantly replied, "Who is the Lord that I should obey Him?" and made the Israelites' slavery even worse.

The Ten Plagues

God unleashed a series of miraculous plagues (Blood, Frogs, Lice, Flies, Livestock, Boils, Hail, Locusts, Darkness, and the Firstborn). These weren't random natural disasters; God was systematically defeating every false god of Egypt to prove He alone is Lord!

The Hardened Heart

Even when Pharaoh's own magicians told him, "This is the finger of God," Pharaoh stubbornly refused to listen. He would fake an apology just to make the pain stop, but his heart never truly changed. His pride eventually destroyed his entire nation.

Mid-Week Dive Deep

Grab a parent, a sibling, or just your Bible and a notebook, and take 10 minutes to work through this!

1. READ IT: Read Exodus 8:15 and Exodus 10:7-9 together.

For Younger Kids:

Why do you think Pharaoh kept changing his mind after the plagues stopped? How can we make sure our hearts stay "soft" and obedient to God, even when it's hard?

For Older Youth:

Pharaoh tried to compromise with God (he said: "You can go, but leave your animals behind"). In what ways do we try to compromise with sin, giving God most of our lives but holding something back for ourselves?

Weekly Action Steps

Choose your challenge for the week:

The "Soft Heart" Challenge

Pharaoh held onto Israel out of pure pride, and it cost him everything. Is there a grudge you're holding onto? An apology you refuse to give? A rule you keep breaking? This week, challenge yourself to swallow your pride and let it go. Don't be like Pharaoh!

The "No Compromise" Audit

What is one area of your life you've left back in "Egypt"? (Your money, your media choices, a toxic friendship?) Make a commitment this week to bring 100% of your life under God's authority. Leave no hoof behind!

The 5-Minute Meditation

Spend 5 minutes in total silence this week thinking about God's massive power over the elements. Ask Him: "God, show me any area where my heart has become hard toward You."
