Out of Egypt

Small Group Discussion & Action Guide | Exodus 1-3

🗣️ Discuss It
1. Moses looked "in all directions" before killing the Egyptian, but he forgot to look UP. Have you ever tried to hide a sin? What happens when we take matters into our own hands?
2. Read Exodus 2:23-25. The Israelites "groaned" under slavery. Why is it comforting to know God hears our groans even before we have the perfect words to pray?
3. When God called Moses from the burning bush, Moses made a ton of excuses. If God asked you to do something big for Him right now, what excuse would you use?
🚀 Do It (Weekly Action Steps)
1. Identify Your "Egypt": What is an area of your life where you feel stuck or enslaved (a bad habit, a toxic friendship, fear)? Write it in your journal and ask God to rescue you.

2. The "I AM" Challenge: When Moses said "I can't," God said, "I AM." Every time you doubt yourself this week, replace your "I can't" with God's promise from Exodus 3:12: "I will be with you."

The Great Debate

Your Stance: MOSES WAS JUSTIFIED

Ammunition for your argument:

  • The Egyptian was brutally beating an innocent, enslaved Hebrew. Moses was protecting the weak.
  • Moses was demonstrating a God-given heart for justice.
  • The Hebrews were facing extreme oppression and genocide (baby boys being thrown in the Nile). Extreme oppression sometimes requires extreme action to save a life.
Tip: Don't just read the bullets. Use them to build your case! Speak clearly and respect the other team.

The Great Debate

Your Stance: MOSES WAS WRONG

Ammunition for your argument:

  • Murder is against God's character. Moses acted in secret (looking both ways), which proves he knew what he was doing was wrong.
  • Moses acted out of his own anger and flesh rather than waiting for God's instruction.
  • His actions didn't actually free his people; it just made him a fugitive in the desert for 40 years!
